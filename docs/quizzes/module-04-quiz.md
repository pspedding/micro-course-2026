## Question 4

*Lesson L01 · Supply Demand*


What is the difference between a change in quantity supplied and a change in supply?


**Type:** Short Answer

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** A change in quantity supplied is a movement along the supply curve caused by a price change. A change in supply is a shift of the entire supply curve caused by a non-price determinant (input costs, technology, expectations, etc.).

    Only price changes cause movements along the curve; everything else shifts the curve.

## Question 9

*Lesson L02 · Supply Demand*


Explain how expectations about future prices can shift current supply.


**Type:** Short Answer

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** If producers expect prices to rise in the future they may withhold supply now to sell later at higher prices, reducing current supply. Conversely, expected price falls may prompt increased current supply.

    Forward-looking behaviour by producers shifts current supply based on expected future conditions.

## Question 13

*Lesson L03 · Supply Demand*


Why do prices tend to fall when there is a surplus in a market?


**Type:** Short Answer

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** When quantity supplied exceeds quantity demanded, producers have unsold inventory. To clear stock, they cut prices. As price falls, quantity demanded rises and quantity supplied falls until the market clears at equilibrium.

    Excess supply creates downward pressure on price — sellers compete to attract buyers.

## Question 19

*Lesson L04 · Supply Demand*


What is comparative statics analysis?


**Type:** Short Answer

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** Comparative statics compares two equilibrium positions — before and after a change in an exogenous variable (like income, input costs, or technology). It predicts the direction of change in equilibrium price and quantity without analysing the adjustment path.

    We compare static equilibria rather than tracing the dynamic adjustment process.

## Question 23

*Lesson L05 · Supply Demand*


When a surplus exists in a market, what adjustment mechanism restores equilibrium?


**Type:** Short Answer

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** With a surplus sellers cannot sell all their output at the current price. They compete for buyers by cutting prices. As price falls demand rises and supply falls until Qd=Qs is restored.

    Price competition among sellers drives prices down to clear the surplus.

## Question 29

*Lesson L06 · Supply Demand*


Explain why market economies generally outperform command economies in resource allocation.


**Type:** Short Answer

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** Market prices aggregate decentralised information about consumer preferences and production costs that no central planner can access.     The invisible hand uses prices to coordinate decentralised decisions more efficiently than central planning.
