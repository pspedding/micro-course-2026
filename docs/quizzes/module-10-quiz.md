## Question 6

The Coase theorem states that private bargaining achieves efficiency if:


**Type:** Multiple Choice

- **C)** Both parties have equal bargaining power
- **D)** The good is a public good

??? success "Show Answer"

## Question 7

**Type:** Calculation

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** Abate pollution; net gain $50k

## Question 8

*Lesson L02 · Externalities*


Why does the Coase theorem often fail in practice for environmental problems like river pollution?


**Type:** Short Answer

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** Transaction costs are high — many affected parties (fishers, residents, recreational users) make negotiation costly. Free-riding on others' negotiations and difficulty measuring damages further impede private bargaining.

    Large numbers of affected parties and high transaction costs prevent the costless bargaining the theorem requires.

## Question 12

*Lesson L03 · Externalities*


A factory's MPC=$40 and external pollution cost=$15/unit. An optimal Pigouvian tax is imposed. What price do consumers pay and what price do producers receive?


**Type:** Calculation

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** Consumers pay $55; producers receive $40 (before tax)

    Tax=$15. Producers set MPC+tax=price: $40+$15=$55. Producers receive $55 but remit $15 tax, netting $40 = their MPC. Efficient outcome.

## Question 13

*Lesson L03 · Externalities*


Why might tradeable permits be preferred over a Pigouvian tax when abatement costs are uncertain?


**Type:** Short Answer

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** With a tax we know the price of emissions but not the quantity. With permits we set the quantity directly — useful when we have a firm emission reduction target. If abatement costs vary across firms, permits also achieve the target cost-effectively through trading.

    Permits give certainty over the quantity of pollution; taxes give certainty over the price.

## Question 23

*Lesson L05 · Externalities*


Why do markets with strong network effects tend toward 'winner-take-all' outcomes?


**Type:** Short Answer

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** Users prefer the platform with the most users (highest network value). This creates a positive feedback loop: more users → more value → more users → dominant platform. Smaller rivals offer less value, making it hard to overcome the incumbent's lead.

    Positive feedback loops from network effects concentrate users on one platform.
