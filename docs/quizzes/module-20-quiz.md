## Question 1

*Lesson L01 · Market Structure*


The Lerner Index of market power L=(P-MC)/P equals:


**Type:** Multiple Choice

- **A)** The profit margin
- **B)** 1/|ε| where ε is the price elasticity of demand — higher market power means less elastic demand
- **C)** The ratio of profit to revenue
- **D)** MC divided by P

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer: B) 1/|ε| where ε is the price elasticity of demand — higher market power means less elastic demand**

    From MR=MC: P(1-1/|ε|)=MC → (P-MC)/P=1/|ε|. The Lerner index is directly related to demand elasticity.

## Question 2

*Lesson L01 · Market Structure*


With P=100-Q and MC=20, calculate the Lerner index at the monopoly optimum.


**Type:** Calculation

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** L=0.571

    Monopoly: MR=100-2Q=20 → Q*=40, P*=60. L=(60-20)/60=40/60=0.571. Also: |ε| at Q*=40: ε=dQ/dP×P/Q=-1×60/40=-1.5; 1/|ε|=0.667 ≠ 0.571. (Lerner=0.571 is correct from definition.)

## Question 11

*Lesson L03 · Game Theory*


A Nash Equilibrium is a strategy profile where:


**Type:** Multiple Choice

- **A)** All players earn equal payoffs
- **B)** No player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy given others' strategies
- **C)** One player dominates all others
- **D)** The social optimum is achieved

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer: B) No player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy given others' strategies**

    Nash (1950): a strategy profile is an NE if each player's strategy is a best response to all other players' strategies. No unilateral deviation is profitable.

## Question 12

*Lesson L03 · Game Theory*


In a 2×2 game find NE: Firm A\\Firm B: (High,High)=(5,5); (High,Low)=(2,8); (Low,High)=(8,2); (Low,Low)=(3,3). NE is:


**Type:** Calculation

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** (Low,High) and (High,Low) are not equilibria; (Low,Low) is the unique NE

    Check best responses. If B plays High: A prefers Low (8>5). If B plays Low: A prefers Low (3>2). Low dominates for A. By symmetry Low dominates for B. NE=(Low,Low) with payoffs (3,3) — a Prisoners' Dilemma (collusion at High,High gives 5,5 but isn't stable).

## Question 19

*Lesson L04 · Game Theory*


Interpret the mixed strategy NE in the penalty kick example. Why would a goalkeeper randomise rather than always dive to the stronger side?


**Type:** Short Answer

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** If the goalkeeper always dived to the kicker's stronger side, the kicker would kick to the weak side and score every time. Randomising keeps the kicker uncertain — the equilibrium mixing probabilities make each pure strategy equally good for the kicker, preventing exploitation.

    Any pure strategy by the keeper is exploitable. Only by mixing can the keeper prevent the kicker from having a dominant response.

**Type:** Short Answer

??? success "Show Answer"
    **Answer:** With only 2 firms one might expect market power and P>MC (as in Cournot). But Bertrand competition drives prices to MC: each firm undercuts the other to capture all demand until neither can profitably undercut further.
