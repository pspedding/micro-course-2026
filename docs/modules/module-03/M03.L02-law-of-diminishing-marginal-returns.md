## Learning Objective

Explain why marginal product eventually declines in the short run.

---

## Diminishing Marginal Returns

In Australian mining operations, the law states that adding more of a variable input (like labour) to fixed inputs (like equipment) will eventually yield smaller increases in output. This occurs because:
1. Initially, workers can specialize (MP increases)
2. Eventually, workers share fixed equipment (MP decreases but positive)
3. Finally, overcrowding occurs (MP becomes negative)

Production has three stages:
- Stage I: MP > AP (AP increasing)
- Stage II: 0 < MP < AP (AP decreasing)
- Stage III: MP < 0 (TP decreasing)

Rational firms operate in Stage II where MP is positive but diminishing.

---

## Worked Example

**Australian Iron Ore Mine**

A Pilbara mine with fixed equipment:
1. 0-3 workers: MP increases (specialization)
2. 4-6 workers: MP positive but decreasing (equipment sharing)
3. 7+ workers: MP negative (overcrowding at crushers)

At 5 workers, TP=100 tonnes, MP=10. Adding a 6th worker gives TP=107, MP=7 (diminishing returns).

---

## Common Misconception

> "Diminishing returns mean the business is failing."

Actually, it's a natural consequence of fixed inputs. Even successful Australian mines experience diminishing returns when adding too many workers to limited equipment.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Applies in short run with fixed inputs
- MP eventually falls due to fixed factors
- Rational production occurs in Stage II
- Different from diseconomies of scale (long-run concept)
