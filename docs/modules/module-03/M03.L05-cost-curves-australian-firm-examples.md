## Australian Industry Applications

**Mining (BHP):**
- High fixed costs (equipment)
- Low marginal costs once producing
- Economies of scale in rail/port infrastructure

**Retail (Coles):**
- U-shaped ATC from high initial inventory costs
- MC low for additional units sold
- Diseconomies in over-expanded stores

**Agriculture (GrainCorp):**
- Seasonal fixed costs (silos)
- MP determines optimal harvest labour
- Transport costs affect MC curves

---

## Worked Example

**Allocative Efficiency: Electricity Market**

When wholesale price = $80/MWh:
1. Snowy Hydro runs hydro plants (MC=$20)
2. Gas plants run (MC=$60)
3. Coal plants idle (MC=$90)

Efficient allocation occurs when P=MC for last unit supplied.

---

## Common Misconception

> "Firms always produce at minimum ATC."

Actually, competitive pressures (like Australian supermarkets) may force production where P=MC, which could be above min ATC.

---

## Key Takeaways

- MC=PL/MPL guides hiring decisions
- P=MC ensures allocative efficiency
- P=min ATC is productive efficiency
- Industry structures shape cost curves
