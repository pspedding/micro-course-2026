## Learning Objective

Explain how entry and exit drive long-run equilibrium in perfect competition.

---

## The Adjustment Process

Perfect competition reaches long-run equilibrium through:

1. **Economic Profit → Entry**
   - New firms enter
   - Market supply increases
   - Price falls until profit = 0

2. **Economic Loss → Exit**
   - Firms exit
   - Market supply decreases
   - Price rises until loss = 0

Australian example: Solar panel installation market
- Early 2010s: High profits → surge of new installers
- By 2020: Prices stabilized → normal profits

---

## Long-Run Conditions

At equilibrium:
- P = MC = min ATC
- Firms earn **normal profit** (zero economic profit)
- Productive efficiency (min ATC)
- Allocative efficiency (P = MC)

Key distinction:
- Economic profit = 0
- Accounting profit > 0 (includes opportunity costs)

---

## Worked Example

**Australian Taxi Industry Post-Uber**

1. Pre-Uber:
   - Limited licenses → high P
   - Economic profits

2. Ride-sharing entry:
   - Supply increases
   - Prices fall
   - Traditional taxis exit

3. Long-run:
   - Normal returns for remaining operators
   - Lower prices for consumers

---

## Common Misconception

> "Zero economic profit means firms aren't making money"

Actually, normal profit means firms earn exactly their opportunity cost - enough to keep them in the industry. Owners still get fair compensation for their work and capital.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Entry/exit eliminate economic profits/losses
- Long-run equilibrium achieves dual efficiency
- Normal profit sustains business activity
- Real-world markets constantly adjust
