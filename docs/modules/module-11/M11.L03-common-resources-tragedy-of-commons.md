## Learning Objective

Analyze how unrestricted access leads to overuse of common resources.

---

## Tragedy of the Commons

Garrett Hardin's (1968) concept explains why shared resources get depleted:
- Each user gains individually from increased use
- Costs of overuse are shared by all
- Rational individuals overconsume

Australian examples:
- **Murray-Darling Basin**: Water allocations exceed sustainable levels
- **Great Barrier Reef**: Overfishing threatens ecosystem
- **Urban roads**: Congestion from unlimited access

---

## Worked Example

**Fisheries Management**
1. Unregulated: Each fisher maximizes catch → stock depletion
2. Solutions:
   - Quotas (individual transferable quotas in Australian fisheries)
   - Licenses (limited commercial fishing permits)
   - Community management (Indigenous ranger programs)

---

## Common Misconception

> "Privatization always solves commons problems"

While privatization can help (e.g., grazing rights), some resources can't be easily divided (e.g., air quality). Elinor Ostrom showed community governance often works better.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Unregulated common resources face overuse
- Individual incentives conflict with group welfare
- Solutions include privatization, regulation, and community management
- Australian examples show real-world applications
