## Learning Objective

Evaluate public projects using cost-benefit analysis principles.

---

## Cost-Benefit Analysis

Governments use CBA to determine efficient provision:
1. Identify all costs and benefits
2. Quantify in monetary terms where possible
3. Discount future values to present terms
4. Compare aggregate benefits to costs

Australian practice:
- **Infrastructure Australia**: Requires CBA for major projects
- **Sydney Metro**: Justified through transport time savings
- **Bruce Highway Upgrade**: Safety benefits quantified

Challenges include valuing non-market goods (e.g., environmental impacts).

---

## Worked Example

**Evaluating a New Hospital**
1. Costs:
   - Construction: $500 million
   - Annual operations: $50 million
2. Benefits:
   - Health outcomes valued at $60 million/year
   - Reduced travel time: $10 million/year
3. Over 30 years (discounted):
   - Present cost: $1.1 billion
   - Present benefit: $1.3 billion
4. Net benefit → Project proceeds

---

## Common Misconception

> "All government spending is wasteful"

CBA provides systematic evaluation. While imperfect, it prevents arbitrary decisions. Australian projects like the Snowy Hydro scheme show successful applications.

---

## Key Takeaways

- CBA compares societal costs and benefits
- Discounting adjusts for time preferences
- Non-market valuation remains challenging
- Australian institutions employ rigorous methods
