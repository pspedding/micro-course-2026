## Learning Objective
Identify the five key determinants that shift the supply curve

---
## Supply Shifters

While price changes cause movement along the supply curve, these five factors cause the entire curve to shift:

1. **Input prices**: Rising costs of production decrease supply. Australian beef supply decreased when drought raised feed costs.
2. **Technology**: Improvements increase supply. Automated mining equipment boosted Australian mineral exports.
3. **Expectations**: Future price expectations affect current supply. Anticipating higher wool prices, farmers may withhold current supply.
4. **Number of sellers**: More producers increase market supply. Entry of craft breweries expanded Australia's beer supply.
5. **Prices of related goods**: If farmers can switch crops, higher prices for alternative crops may reduce supply of the original.

---
## Worked Example

**Australian Coal Supply Shift**

1. Baseline: Supply curve S1 at 100 million tonnes/year
2. New carbon tax increases production costs → supply decreases to S2 (80 million tonnes)
3. Simultaneously, new mining technology is adopted → supply increases to S3 (90 million tonnes)
4. Net effect: Supply shifts left from S1 to S3 (10% decrease)

---
## Common Misconception

> "Bad weather always reduces supply"

While droughts reduce agricultural supply, ideal weather can increase it. The direction depends on the specific conditions.

---
## Key Takeaways

- Supply shifts require changing the entire supply schedule
- Input costs and technology are most impactful in the long run
- Multiple shifts can occur simultaneously
- Australian examples demonstrate real-world applications
