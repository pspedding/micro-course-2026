## Learning Objective
Determine equilibrium price and quantity using supply and demand curves

---
## Finding Equilibrium

Market equilibrium occurs where quantity supplied equals quantity demanded (Qd = Qs). At this intersection point:
- There is no tendency for price to change
- The market "clears" with no surplus or shortage
- Both buyers and sellers are satisfied

In Australia's housing market, equilibrium prices emerge from the interaction of:
- Supply: Available properties, construction costs
- Demand: Population growth, interest rates, investor activity

---
## Worked Example

**Sydney Apartment Market**

1. Demand function: Qd = 500,000 - 2,000P
2. Supply function: Qs = 100,000 + 3,000P
3. Set Qd = Qs → 500,000 - 2,000P = 100,000 + 3,000P
4. Solve: 400,000 = 5,000P → P* = $80,000 per apartment
5. Q* = 100,000 + 3,000(80) = 340,000 apartments

---
## Common Misconception

> "Equilibrium means equal numbers of buyers and sellers"

Equilibrium means quantities supplied and demanded are equal at a given price, not that the number of participants is equal.

---
## Key Takeaways

- Equilibrium is the price-quantity combination where markets clear
- Graphical analysis shows the intersection point
- Algebraic solutions provide precise values
- Australian housing demonstrates real-world equilibrium dynamics
