## Learning Objective
Predict price and quantity changes from shifts in supply or demand

---
## Four Fundamental Shifts

1. **Demand increases**: Higher price, higher quantity (COVID toilet paper demand)
2. **Demand decreases**: Lower price, lower quantity (Declining newspaper demand)
3. **Supply increases**: Lower price, higher quantity (Australian iron ore expansion)
4. **Supply decreases**: Higher price, lower quantity (Drought-reduced crop yields)

During COVID-19, Australian food markets experienced simultaneous:
- Demand increase (panic buying)
- Supply decrease (logistics disruptions)
Result: Sharp price spikes with ambiguous quantity effects

---
## Worked Example

**Iron Ore Boom 2021**

1. Baseline: P1 = $100/tonne, Q1 = 1 million tonnes
2. Chinese demand increases → demand curve shifts right
3. Australian supply decreases (weather disruptions) → supply shifts left
4. Result: P2 = $180/tonne (price definitely rises)
   Q2 ambiguous (depends on relative shift magnitudes)

---
## Common Misconception

> "When both curves shift, price and quantity always move in predictable directions"

With simultaneous shifts, one variable's change may be ambiguous depending on relative shift magnitudes.

---
## Key Takeaways

- Single shifts have clear price-quantity effects
- Double shifts require analyzing relative magnitudes
- Australian examples demonstrate complex real-world scenarios
- Iron ore and agriculture show export market dynamics

---
## Practice

1. How did COVID border closures affect Australia's fruit markets?
2. What happens to price when demand increases and supply increases more?
3. Why might wheat prices fall despite a poor harvest?
