## Learning Objective
Explain how prices coordinate economic activity through signals

---
## Price Mechanism

Prices serve three key functions:

1. **Signaling**: High prices indicate scarcity, low prices indicate abundance
2. **Incentivizing**: Profit opportunities attract resources, losses repel them
3. **Rationing**: Allocate goods to those willing/able to pay

During Australia's 2020 bushfires:
- Timber prices rose → signaled shortage
- Attracted imports and alternative materials
- Rationed remaining stock to highest-value uses

---
## Worked Example

**Australian Lithium Market**

1. 2015: Price = $5,000/tonne → moderate production
2. 2018: EV demand spikes → price rises to $12,000
3. Signal attracts:
   - New mines (Greenbushes expansion)
   - Recycling technologies
   - Exploration investment
4. By 2022: Increased supply → price stabilizes at $8,000

---
## Common Misconception

> "Prices reflect only production costs"

Prices reflect both production costs and consumer valuations through the interaction of supply and demand.

---
## Key Takeaways

- Prices communicate information throughout the economy
- Profit/loss signals guide resource allocation
- Market economies self-correct through price adjustments
- Australian resource markets demonstrate dynamic adjustment
