## Learning Objective
Distinguish between shortages and surpluses in market disequilibrium

---
## Price Controls and Disequilibrium

When prices are prevented from reaching equilibrium:

**Shortage (excess demand)**:
- Occurs when price is below equilibrium
- Quantity demanded exceeds quantity supplied
- Example: Sydney housing with rent controls

**Surplus (excess supply)**:
- Occurs when price is above equilibrium
- Quantity supplied exceeds quantity demanded
- Example: Australian milk before dairy deregulation

---
## Worked Example

**Australian Rental Market**

1. Equilibrium rent: $500/week for 100,000 properties
2. Government caps rent at $400 → landlords supply 80,000
3. At $400, demand is 120,000 → shortage of 40,000 properties
4. Results: Waiting lists, black markets, reduced maintenance

---
## Common Misconception

> "Shortages mean there isn't enough of a product"

Shortages occur when quantity demanded exceeds supplied at a given price - the product exists but isn't accessible at that price.

---
## Key Takeaways

- Shortages occur below equilibrium price
- Surpluses occur above equilibrium price
- Both create market inefficiencies
- Australian examples show policy impacts
