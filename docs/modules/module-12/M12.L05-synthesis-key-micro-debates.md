## The Microeconomic Framework Revisited

Our journey through microeconomics has built a systematic way to analyze choices under scarcity:

1. **Scarcity → PPF**: Tradeoffs define production possibilities
2. **Markets**: Supply/demand allocate resources efficiently
3. **Market Failure**: When markets don't achieve efficiency
   - Market power (monopolies)
   - Externalities (pollution)
   - Public goods (national defense)
   - Common resources (fisheries)
   - Information asymmetry (used cars)
4. **Government Role**: Correcting failures while managing equity-efficiency tradeoffs

---

## Worked Example

**Housing Affordability Debate**

1. **Market Perspective**:
   - High prices signal scarcity (supply < demand)
   - Zoning laws restrict supply (market failure)
   - Negative externalities from homelessness

2. **Policy Responses**:
   - Upzoning to increase supply (efficiency)
   - Rent control (equity but efficiency costs)
   - First-home buyer grants (demand-side, may raise prices)

3. **Australian Context**:
   - Sydney/Melbourne prices rose 150% 2009-2022
   - Debate over local vs. state planning controls

---

## Common Misconception

> "Government intervention always reduces economic efficiency"

Well-designed policies can correct market failures and increase total surplus. Australian emissions policy (Safeguard Mechanism) aims to internalize carbon externalities without abandoning market mechanisms.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Microeconomics provides tools to analyze policy tradeoffs
- Market failures justify targeted interventions
- Australian debates (housing, climate) reflect core micro concepts
- Economic analysis informs but doesn't dictate policy choices
