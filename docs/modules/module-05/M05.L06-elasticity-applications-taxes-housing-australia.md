## Learning Objective

Analyse tax incidence and deadweight loss using elasticity in Australian markets.

---

## Tax Incidence and Elasticity

Tax burden falls more heavily on the inelastic side of the market (buyers or sellers). Deadweight loss (DWL) occurs when taxes reduce TS below its maximum.

**Australian examples:**
- **Tobacco tax:** Inelastic demand → consumers bear most burden.
- **Fuel excise:** Inelastic demand → minimal quantity change, high revenue.
- **Housing:** Inelastic supply in Sydney → taxes raise prices more than quantity.

---

## Worked Example

**Tax Incidence in the Sydney Housing Market**

1. **Pre-tax:** Equilibrium price = $1M, quantity = 1,000 homes.
2. **Tax:** $50k levy on sellers (supply shifts left).
3. **Post-tax:** Buyers pay $1.04M, sellers receive $0.99M, quantity = 950 homes.
4. **Burden:** Buyers pay 80% of tax ($40k), sellers 20% ($10k) due to inelastic supply.
5. **DWL:** Triangle area = \( \frac{1}{2} \times 50 \times (1.04M - 0.99M) \) = $1.25M.

---

## Common Misconception

> "Taxes always hurt sellers more than buyers."

Tax burden depends on relative elasticities, not who physically pays the tax.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Tax incidence is split based on relative elasticities.
- DWL is larger when elasticity is high.
- Sydney housing taxes show high buyer burden due to inelastic supply.
- Fuel and tobacco taxes exploit inelastic demand.
