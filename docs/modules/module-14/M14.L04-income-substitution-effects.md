## Learning Objective

Decompose price effects into income and substitution components.

---

## Effect Decomposition

When p₁ falls:
1. **Substitution Effect (SE):** Movement along IC to new MRS (always negative)
2. **Income Effect (IE):** Parallel shift of budget line

For normal goods: SE & IE reinforce (↓p₁ ⇒ ↑x₁)
For inferior goods: SE & IE oppose (Giffen if IE dominates)

Australian example: Rice is inferior (↑income ⇒ ↓consumption), while quinoa is normal.

---

## Worked Example

**Brisbane Transport Choices**

Initial: p₁=$5 (bus), p₂=$20 (Uber), m=$200, U=x₁x₂
Optimal: x₁*=20, x₂*=5

After bus fare cut to p₁'=$4:
1. New optimal: x₁'=25, x₂'=5
2. SE: Find bundle at new prices with original utility (U=100)
   Solve x₁x₂=100 s.t. 4x₁+20x₂=200 → x₁ˢ≈22.36, x₂ˢ≈4.47
3. SE = 22.36 - 20 = +2.36
4. IE = 25 - 22.36 = +2.64

---

## Common Misconception

> "Giffen goods violate the law of demand"

Giffen goods (like cheap staples during Australian gold rushes) are rare but don't violate theory - they're inferior goods where IE > SE.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Substitution effect always opposes price change
- Income effect direction depends on good type
- Slutsky equation: Total Effect = SE + IE
- Hicksian decomposition holds utility constant
- Australian Giffen candidates: cheap bread, milk in recessions
