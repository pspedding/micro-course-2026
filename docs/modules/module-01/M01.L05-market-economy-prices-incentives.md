## Learning Objective

Explain how prices coordinate economic activity.

---

## Market Coordination

In market economies like Australia's, prices serve three vital functions:
1. **Signals**: High iron ore prices tell miners to increase production
2. **Incentives**: Rising coffee shop profits encourage new competitors
3. **Rationing**: During droughts, higher water prices reduce consumption

Adam Smith's "invisible hand" describes how self-interested actions (Woolworths maximizing profits) create social benefits (food availability) through price mechanisms.

---

## Worked Example

**Australian Housing Market**

1. Rising demand (population growth) increases prices
2. Higher prices signal developers to build more homes
3. Construction companies hire more workers (derived demand)
4. Eventually, increased supply moderates price growth
5. Government interventions (first-home grants) can distort these signals

---

## Common Misconception

> "Markets always produce fair outcomes"

Markets efficiently allocate resources but may produce unequal results. Australia's ACCC exists to correct market failures like monopolies (e.g., regulating airport fees).

---

## Key Takeaways

- Prices communicate information about scarcity and value
- Profit incentives drive innovation and efficiency
- "Dollar votes" determine what gets produced in market systems
- Governments may intervene to address market failures or equity concerns
