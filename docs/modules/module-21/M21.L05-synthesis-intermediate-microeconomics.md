## Core Framework

Intermediate microeconomics builds from fundamentals:

1. Preferences → Choice → Demand
2. Technology → Costs → Supply
3. Market Equilibrium → Welfare
4. Market Failures:
   - Monopoly power
   - Externalities
   - Public goods
   - Asymmetric information
   - Incomplete markets

Information economics adds sixth market failure dimension:

- Adverse selection
- Moral hazard
- Signalling/screening
- Mechanism design

---

## Australian Context

Key applications in Australian economy:

1. ACCC regulation of monopolies
2. Carbon pricing for externalities
3. Medicare addressing adverse selection
4. HECS/HELP as signalling mechanism
5. AEMO electricity market design

Modern extensions:

- Behavioral insights in consumer policy
- Platform market competition
- Matching markets in education

## Worked Example

**[Australian context]**

Analyze Australian private health insurance through microeconomic lens:

1. Demand: Risk preferences affect uptake
2. Supply: Insurers face moral hazard
3. Equilibrium: Adverse selection requires policy response
4. Government intervention: Medicare Levy Surcharge

---

## Common Misconception

> "Market failures mean markets don't work"

Correction: Market failures identify specific conditions where markets may not achieve efficiency, not that markets generally fail.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Microeconomics provides unified analytical framework
- Six market failure types require different solutions
- Australian economy offers rich application examples
- Frontier topics extend traditional models
