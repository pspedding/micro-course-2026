## Learning Objective

Identify the five determinants of demand and analyze their impact on demand shifts.

---

## Demand Shifters

1. **Income:** For normal goods (e.g., Sydney housing), demand rises with income. For inferior goods (e.g., instant noodles), demand falls.
2. **Prices of Related Goods:** Substitutes (e.g., Uber vs. taxis) and complements (e.g., smartphones/data plans).
3. **Tastes:** Trends (e.g., plant-based meat demand growth).
4. **Expectations:** Future price changes (e.g., pre-GST hike spending spikes).
5. **Number of Buyers:** Population growth boosts demand (e.g., Melbourne apartments).

---

## Worked Example

**Melbourne Cafés During COVID**

1. Income drops → demand falls (inferior good: home coffee).
2. Fewer tourists → fewer buyers.
3. Expectation of lockdowns → panic-bought coffee machines.
4. Result: Leftward demand curve shift.

---

## Common Misconception

> "A price change shifts the demand curve."

Price changes cause movements along the curve; only non-price factors shift it.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Income, related goods, tastes, expectations, and buyer count shift demand.
- Normal vs. inferior goods react differently to income changes.
- Comparative statics compare equilibria before/after a shift.
