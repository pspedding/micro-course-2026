## Learning Objective

Construct a market demand curve by summing individual demand curves horizontally.

---

## Market Demand Construction

Market demand aggregates individual demands at each price. Example: Australian wheat farmers sell to domestic and international buyers.

**Horizontal Summation Steps:**
1. List quantities demanded by each buyer at set prices.
2. Sum quantities at each price.
3. Plot the summed quantities.

**Kink at Choke Price:** The price where some buyers drop out (e.g., luxury car imports).

---

## Worked Example

**Brisbane Organic Farmers' Market**

1. Buyer A: 2kg at $10, 1kg at $15.
2. Buyer B: 3kg at $10, 0kg at $15.
3. Market demand: 5kg at $10, 1kg at $15.
4. Curve kinks at $15 (Buyer B exits).

---

## Common Misconception

> "Market demand is just the average of individual demands."

Summing is additive, not averaged, reflecting total quantity demanded.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Market demand is the horizontal sum of individual demands.
- Kinks occur at choke prices where buyers exit.
- Aggregation applies to commodities (e.g., iron ore exports).

---

## Practice
