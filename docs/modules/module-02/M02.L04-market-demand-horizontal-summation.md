## Learning Objective

Construct a market demand curve by summing individual demand curves horizontally.

---

## Market Demand Construction

Market demand aggregates individual demands at each price. Example: Australian wheat farmers sell to domestic and international buyers.

**Horizontal Summation Steps:**
1. List quantities demanded by each buyer at set prices.
2. Sum quantities at each price.
3. Plot the summed quantities.

**Kink at Choke Price:** The price where some buyers drop out (e.g., luxury car imports).

---

## Worked Example

**Brisbane Organic Farmers' Market**

1. Buyer A: 2kg at $10, 1kg at $15.
2. Buyer B: 3kg at $10, 0kg at $15.
3. Market demand: 5kg at $10, 1kg at $15.
4. Curve kinks at $15 (Buyer B exits).

---

## Common Misconception

> "Market demand is just the average of individual demands."

Summing is additive, not averaged, reflecting total quantity demanded.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Market demand is the horizontal sum of individual demands.
- Kinks occur at choke prices where buyers exit.
- Aggregation applies to commodities (e.g., iron ore exports).

---

## Exam-Style Calculation Practice

!!! tip "Key technique: convert to Q = f(P), sum, then invert back to P = f(Q)"
    When summing individual demand curves algebraically: (1) rewrite each as quantity demanded, (2) add quantities, (3) invert the result to get the market inverse demand.

**Example:** Two consumers of concert tickets:
- Anna: p = 40 − q → q_A = 40 − p (valid for p ≤ 40)
- Boris: p = 80 − 4q → q_B = 20 − p/4 (valid for p ≤ 80)

*For prices between 40 and 80 only Boris buys:* Q = q_B = 20 − p/4 → **p = 80 − 4Q**

*For prices below 40 both buy:*
Q = q_A + q_B = (40 − p) + (20 − p/4) = 60 − 5p/4

Invert: 5p/4 = 60 − Q → p = 48 − (4/5)Q → **market demand: p = 48 − 0.8Q**

Note the **kink at p = 40** where Anna enters the market — the curve becomes flatter below that price.

## Practice

1. Two buyers: A has p = 30 − 2q; B has p = 50 − q. Derive the market demand curve for prices below 30. (*Hint: convert each to Q, sum, invert.*)
2. Sum demands for two Sydney cyclists: A (1 bike at $500, 0 at $600) and B (2 at $500, 1 at $600).
3. For the Anna/Boris example above, verify that at p = 20 total market demand Q = 40 − 20 + 20 − 5 = 35.
