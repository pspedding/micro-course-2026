## Learning Objective

Apply demand analysis to Australian market case studies.

---

## Case 1: Sydney Housing Demand

**Drivers:** Population growth, low interest rates, foreign investment. Demand is price inelastic (limited substitutes). COVID-19 shifted preferences to suburbs.

---

## Case 2: Electricity Demand

**Inelasticity:** Essential service; price hikes (e.g., 2022 energy crisis) reduce consumption minimally. Time-of-use pricing attempts to shift peak demand.

---

## Case 3: Food Delivery Platforms

**Demand Shifters:** Pandemic lockdowns (↑), reopening (↓). Uber Eats competes with Deliveroo; cross-price elasticity matters.

---

## Case 4: Iron Ore Exports

**China Demand:** Price volatility tied to Chinese construction. Australian miners face export demand kinks at price thresholds.

---

## Common Misconception

> "Inelastic demand means demand doesn’t change."

Inelastic means quantity changes less than price (% terms), not zero change.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Sydney housing: Inelastic, shifted by demographics.
- Electricity: Essential but modifiable via pricing.
- Food delivery: Elastic to substitutes and income.
- Iron ore: Export demand drives price sensitivity.

---

## Practice

1. Why is Sydney housing demand inelastic?
2. How might solar adoption affect electricity demand elasticity?
3. Compare COVID impacts on food delivery vs. iron ore demand.
