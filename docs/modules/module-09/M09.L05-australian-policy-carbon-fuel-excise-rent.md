## Learning Objective

Compare the effectiveness of different policy instruments in addressing market failures.

---

## Carbon Pricing in Australia

Australia’s carbon tax (2012-2014) and current Safeguard Mechanism aim to reduce emissions by internalizing the social cost of carbon. The tax faced political opposition but was economically efficient. Fuel excise (~49c/L, indexed to CPI) similarly corrects for road-use externalities. Rent Assistance (a direct payment) avoids the pitfalls of rent control by subsidizing tenants without distorting the housing market.

---

## Worked Example

**Carbon Tax vs. Cap-and-Trade**

1. **Carbon tax:** Sets a fixed price per ton of emissions, creating price certainty but uncertain quantity reductions.
2. **Cap-and-trade:** Sets a fixed emissions cap, creating quantity certainty but volatile prices.
3. **Australian experience:** The carbon tax reduced emissions but was repealed due to political opposition.

---

## Common Misconception

> **"All price regulations are equally effective."**

Policies vary in efficiency: taxes/subsidies can be precise but unpopular, while direct payments (e.g., Rent Assistance) avoid market distortions.

---

## Key Takeaways

- Carbon pricing internalizes environmental costs but faces political challenges.
- Fuel excise corrects for road-use externalities.
- Rent Assistance avoids the inefficiencies of rent control.
- Policy design balances efficiency and equity.
